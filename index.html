Analytic Solution to Heat Equation

The heat equation is given by: $$ \frac{\partial U(x,t)}{\partial t} = \kappa \frac{\partial^2 U(x,t)}{\partial x^2} $$ Suppose the initial and (Dirichlet) boundary conditions are given by: $$ U(x,0) = \sin(x), \quad U(0,t) = U(L,t) = 0 $$ We solve this using the method of separation of variables by assuming a solution of the form: $$ U(x,t) = X(x) T(t) $$ Substituting into the heat equation: $$ X(x) T'(t) = \kappa T(t) X''(x) $$ Dividing both sides by \( X(x) T(t) \) gives: $$ \frac{T'(t)}{\kappa T(t)} = \frac{X''(x)}{X(x)} $$ Since the left side has explicit temporal dependence on time [\(t\)], and the right side has explicit spatial dependence on [\( x \)], both must be equal to a constant, say \( -\lambda \): $$ \frac{T'(t)}{\kappa T(t)} = \frac{X''(x)}{X(x)} = -\lambda $$ Solving for \( X(x) \): $$ X''(x) + \lambda X(x) = 0 $$ The boundary conditions are \( X(0) = 0 \) and \( X(L) = 0 \). This is a standard Sturm-Liouville problem whose solutions are: $$ X_n(x) = \sin \left( \frac{n \pi x}{L} \right) $$ where \( \lambda_n = \left( \frac{n \pi}{L} \right)^2 \) for \( n = 1, 2, 3, \dots \) $$$$ Solving for \( T(t) \): $$ T'(t) + \kappa T(t) = 0 $$ Substituting \( \lambda_n \), the solution to the ODE is: $$ T_n(t) = e^{-\kappa \left( \frac{n \pi}{L} \right)^2 t} $$ The general solution is a sum of the solutions for each \( n \): $$ U(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n \sin \left( \frac{n \pi x}{L} \right) e^{-\kappa \left( \frac{n \pi}{L} \right)^2 t} $$ We apply the initial condition \( U(x,0) = \sin(x) \). This gives: $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n \sin \left( \frac{n \pi x}{L} \right) = \sin(x) $$ The initial condition can be satisfied by choosing the coefficient \( A_1 = 1 \) and all other \( A_n = 0 \), since the function \( \sin(x) \) corresponds to the first mode (\( n = 1 \)). Thus, the solution is: $$ \boxed{ U(x,t) = \sin \left( \frac{\pi x}{L} \right) e^{-\kappa \left( \frac{\pi}{L} \right)^2 t} } $$
