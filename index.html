Analytic Solution to 1D Heat Equation

The heat equation is a partial differential equation that describes how heat diffuses through a given region over time. The 1D heat equation is given by:

$$ \frac{\partial U(x,t)}{\partial t} = \kappa \frac{\partial^2 U(x,t)}{\partial x^2} $$

Where:

  • \( U(x,t) \) is the temperature at position \( x \) and time \( t \),
  • \( \kappa \) is the thermal diffusivity constant.

Suppose the initial and (Dirichlet) boundary conditions are given by: $$ U(x,0) = \sin(x), \quad U(0,t) = U(L,t) = 0 $$ We solve this using the method of separation of variables by assuming a solution of the form: $$ U(x,t) = X(x) T(t) $$ Substituting into the heat equation: $$ X(x) T'(t) = \kappa T(t) X''(x) $$ Dividing both sides by \( X(x) T(t) \) gives: $$ \frac{T'(t)}{\kappa T(t)} = \frac{X''(x)}{X(x)} $$ Since the left side has explicit temporal dependence on time [\(t\)], and the right side has explicit spatial dependence on [\( x \)], both must be equal to a constant, say \( -\lambda \): $$ \frac{T'(t)}{\kappa T(t)} = \frac{X''(x)}{X(x)} = -\lambda $$ Solving for \( X(x) \): $$ X''(x) + \lambda X(x) = 0 $$ The boundary conditions are \( X(0) = 0 \) and \( X(L) = 0 \). This is a standard Sturm-Liouville problem whose solutions are: $$ X_n(x) = \sin \left( \frac{n \pi x}{L} \right) $$ where \( \lambda_n = \left( \frac{n \pi}{L} \right)^2 \) for \( n = 1, 2, 3, \dots \) $$$$ Solving for \( T(t) \): $$ T'(t) + \kappa T(t) = 0 $$ Substituting \( \lambda_n \), the solution to the ODE is: $$ T_n(t) = e^{-\kappa \left( \frac{n \pi}{L} \right)^2 t} $$ The general solution is a sum of the solutions for each \( n \): $$ U(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n \sin \left( \frac{n \pi x}{L} \right) e^{-\kappa \left( \frac{n \pi}{L} \right)^2 t} $$ We apply the initial condition \( U(x,0) = \sin(x) \). This gives: $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n \sin \left( \frac{n \pi x}{L} \right) = \sin(x) $$ The initial condition can be satisfied by choosing the coefficient \( A_1 = 1 \) and all other \( A_n = 0 \), since the function \( \sin(x) \) corresponds to the first mode (\( n = 1 \)). Thus, the solution is: $$ \boxed{ U(x,t) = \sin \left( \frac{\pi x}{L} \right) e^{-\kappa \left( \frac{\pi}{L} \right)^2 t} } $$

Finite Difference Solution to 1D Heat Equation

To solve this equation numerically, we discretize both space and time. We define the following grid points:

  • Space points: \( x_0, x_1, \dots, x_{Nx-1} \), with step size \( \Delta x \),
  • Time points: \( t_0, t_1, \dots, t_{Nt-1} \), with time step size \( \Delta t \).

The finite difference approximation of the spatial derivative is given by the central difference formula:

$$ \frac{\partial^2 U(x,t)}{\partial x^2} \approx \frac{U(x_{i+1}, t) - 2U(x_i, t) + U(x_{i-1}, t)}{(\Delta x)^2} $$

The time derivative is approximated using the forward difference:

$$ \frac{\partial U(x,t)}{\partial t} \approx \frac{U(x, t+\Delta t) - U(x, t)}{\Delta t} $$

Substituting these approximations into the heat equation, we obtain the following update rule:

$$ \frac{U(x, t+\Delta t) - U(x, t)}{\Delta t} = \kappa \frac{U(x_{i+1}, t) - 2U(x_i, t) + U(x_{i-1}, t)}{(\Delta x)^2} $$

Rearranging for \( U(x, t+\Delta t) \), we get the central difference update formula:

$$ U(x_i, t+\Delta t) = U(x_i, t) + \alpha \left( U(x_{i+1}, t) - 2U(x_i, t) + U(x_{i-1}, t) \right) $$

Where \( \alpha = \frac{\kappa \Delta t}{(\Delta x)^2} \) is the stability parameter that governs the time step and spatial resolution. For stability, we safely choose \( \alpha \leq \frac{1}{2} \).

The boundary conditions for this problem are Dirichlet boundary conditions, which are specified as:

  • \( U(0, t) = 0 \) (temperature at the left boundary is zero),
  • \( U(L, t) = 0 \) (temperature at the right boundary is zero).

With this, we can now compute the temperature distribution over time by iteratively applying the update formula for each spatial point at each time step.
